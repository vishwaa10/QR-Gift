Listening to Baby's Heartbeat

A gentle guide to using your stethoscope

✦ Getting Started
📅

When to Start Listening

With a standard stethoscope you can typically hear baby's heartbeat around 18–20 weeks. Earlier detection is easier with a Doppler. Be patient — it takes a little practice and it is so worth it!

🤫

Find a Quiet Space

Choose a calm, silent room — turn off the TV, fans, and phone notifications. Even fabric rustling can interfere. Early morning is ideal when baby tends to be calmer and sounds are clearest.

🛋️

Get Comfortable First

Lie on your back or recline on a firm surface and let your belly muscles fully relax. Even slight tension makes sounds harder to pick up. Take three slow deep breaths before you begin.

✦ Finding the Heartbeat
🎯

Where to Place the Chest Piece

Start just above your pubic bone and slowly move upward. Baby's heart sits in the lower abdomen early on and moves higher as baby grows. Use gentle, firm, steady pressure at all times.

💓

What It Sounds Like

A baby's heartbeat is a rapid galloping rhythm — much faster than yours. A healthy fetal heart rate is 110–160 beats per minute. It is truly one of the most magical sounds you will ever hear.

🔄

Move Slowly and Methodically

If you don't find it straight away, don't panic — move the chest piece in small slow circles. Try tilting it at different angles too. Small adjustments make a huge difference in the clarity of sound.

✦ Things to Keep in Mind
🍃

Don't Confuse with Other Sounds

You may hear your own slow steady pulse, stomach gurgling, or whooshing blood flow. Baby's heartbeat is noticeably faster and rhythmic. With practice you'll tell them apart instantly.

⏱️

Keep Sessions Short and Sweet

Limit listening to 5–10 minutes. Searching too long creates unnecessary anxiety. If you are ever worried — call your midwife or doctor. They have professional Dopplers and are always happy to help.

Your Pregnancy Journey

Little reminders, straight from the heart — just for you, mama 💛

🌿 Taking Care of Your Body
💧

Drink Water Like It's Your Job

Water is doing SO much for you and baby — building amniotic fluid, carrying nutrients, flushing toxins. Aim for 8–10 glasses daily. Keep a cute bottle with you everywhere. You will genuinely feel the difference.

🥗

Eat the Rainbow (Really!)

Don't stress about eating "perfectly" — just add colour to every meal. Leafy greens, orange veggies, berries, lentils. Your baby is literally building organs from what you eat. And yes, the occasional chocolate? Absolutely deserved. 😄

💊

Prenatal Vitamins — Every Single Day

Take them with food if nausea is rough, or try a gummy version. Folic acid, iron, and DHA are the big three — quietly doing incredible work for baby's brain and spine every day, even when you can't see it.

🚶‍♀️

Move a Little Every Day

You don't need a gym. A gentle 20-minute walk reduces swelling, lifts your mood, helps sleep, and prepares your body for labour. Slow yoga on your living room floor counts. Movement is medicine.

😴

Sleep is Sacred — Protect It

Your body is building a human. It needs rest. Sleep on your left side for better blood flow to baby, use a pillow between your knees, and never feel guilty about napping. Rest is productive right now.

🦶

Swollen Feet? Elevate and Breathe

Totally normal and annoying — I know! Elevate your legs when sitting, avoid long standing, wear supportive shoes. A gentle foot massage at the end of the day? Yes — and you deserve to ask for one! 💆‍♀️

🧘‍♀️ Your Mind Matters Too
🧠

Pregnancy Brain is Real

Forgetting words mid-sentence? Losing your keys constantly? Welcome to pregnancy brain — it's a genuine hormonal thing! Write lists, set reminders, and laugh about it. Your brain is reorganising itself into a mama brain. Beautiful.

😢

It's Okay to Not Be Okay

Pregnancy can be overwhelming, emotional, and scary — and nobody talks about that enough. If you feel anxious, weepy, or just not yourself, please reach out. To me, to your doctor, to anyone. Your mental health matters just as much.

📵

Step Away from Scary Internet Holes

I say this with love — Googling symptoms at 2am is never a good idea. Trust your doctor, trust your body. If something genuinely worries you, call your midwife. That is exactly what they are there for.

🌬️

Your Secret Breathing Ritual

When things feel too much — breathe in for 4 counts, hold for 4, out for 6. Do it three times. It genuinely calms your nervous system in under a minute. Always available, always free, always yours.

📋 Practical Things Worth Knowing
📅

Never Miss Your Antenatal Appointments

These check-ups are your window into baby's world. Growth scans, blood tests, glucose checks — they catch things early when everything is still manageable. Put them in your calendar. Non-negotiable, always.

🧳

Pack Your Hospital Bag Early

Start at 34–35 weeks — not the last minute! Think: good snacks, birth plan, cosy robe, charger, baby's first outfit, documents. Having it ready early means one less thing to panic about on the big day. 🎉

🛁

Moisturise That Beautiful Bump

Your belly is stretching in ways it never has before — moisturise daily with a good oil or cream (coconut oil is wonderful!). The daily massage is also a beautiful quiet moment of connection with your bump.

🐟

Know What to Avoid on Your Plate

Raw fish, unpasteurised cheeses, deli meats, undercooked eggs — skip these for now. It is not forever, just these precious months. Your meals can still be delicious and exciting with a few smart swaps.

🫀

Count Baby's Kicks

From about 28 weeks, notice your baby's movement patterns. You should feel at least 10 movements in 2 hours during active times. If baby's quieter than usual — drink cold water, lie down, and focus. Trust your instincts, mama.

🦷

Yes, Even Your Teeth Matter

Hormones make gums more sensitive during pregnancy. Keep up with brushing, flossing, and book a dental check — it's completely safe and recommended. Gum health is more connected to baby's wellbeing than most people realise.
